  • Relish

    Definition : appetizer, hors d'oeuvre Relish is an alteration of Middle English reles, meaning "taste." The word is occasionally encountered to indicate enjoyment in something that satisfies one's tastes—for example, "The hungry teenagers ate with great relish" or "The villain took relish in explaining his fiendish plan." However, relish is most familiar as a word for something adding a zestful flavor, like the food condiment made of chopped pickles. This flavorful sense of relish influenced its use for a savory appetizer.

  • Pupu

    Definition : an Asian dish served as an appetizer or main course and consisting of a variety of foods (such as egg rolls, spareribs, and fried shrimp) Pupu is a borrowing of Hawaiian pūpū, meaning "appetizer." The "pupu platter" is a common offering on American Chinese and Hawaiian menus and usually consists of such items as egg rolls, spareribs, fried shrimp, and chicken. We recommend ordering this appetizer for two or more ... or as a meal for one.

  • Whet

    Definition : appetizer; also : a drink of liquor Whet goes back to Old English as a verb referring to sharpening or putting a sharp edge or point on an object. The noun form appears in the 17th century for things that literally sharpen, or that figuratively sharpen by inciting or stimulating. A whet of food or drink to stimulate the desire to eat is always appreciated, and not to be passed over.
